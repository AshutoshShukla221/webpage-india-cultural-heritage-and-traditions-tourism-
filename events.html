Diwali (Festival of Lights) - Oct/Nov

Diwali, the Hindu festival of lights, is the largest and most celebrated festival in India. This five-day festival symbolizes the spiritual "victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance." Homes are decorated with clay lamps (diyas) and colorful rangoli artworks, families share sweets and gifts, and fireworks light up the night sky, creating a joyous and luminous atmosphere across the entire country.

Holi (Festival of Colors) - March

Holi is a vibrant and exuberant Hindu festival that marks the arrival of spring. Known as the festival of colors, it's a day of pure fun and frolic, where people chase each other with dry powder (gulal) and colored water. The festival signifies the victory of good over evil, the end of winter, and for many, a festive day to meet others, play and laugh, forget and forgive, and repair broken relationships.

Durga Puja - Sep/Oct

Durga Puja is a ten-day Hindu festival, particularly celebrated with grandeur in West Bengal. It reveres the goddess Durga's victory over the demon Mahishasur. The festival is a cultural extravaganza, with cities adorned with elaborate temporary temples called pandals, housing beautifully crafted idols of the goddess. It's a time for art, culture, feasting, and community celebration, culminating in the immersion of the idols in a river.

Pushkar Camel Fair - November

The Pushkar Camel Fair is a captivating annual five-day camel and livestock fair held in the town of Pushkar in Rajasthan. It is one of the world's largest camel fairs and a major tourist attraction. Besides the trading of livestock, it features a host of cultural events, competitions such as the "longest mustache," camel races, and performances by local musicians and dancers, offering a unique glimpse into the vibrant culture of the Rajasthani desert.

Ganesh Chaturthi - Aug/Sep

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrating the arrival of Lord Ganesha to earth from Kailash Parvat. The festival is marked with the installation of Ganesha clay idols privately in homes, or publicly on elaborate pandals. The celebrations last for up to ten days, filled with prayers, music, and dance, concluding with the immersion of the idol in a body of water, symbolizing Ganesha's return to the heavens.

Christmas - December

Christmas is celebrated with great enthusiasm in many parts of India. In states with large Christian populations like Goa, Kerala, and Mizoram, the festival is a major event. Churches hold midnight masses, homes are decorated with Christmas trees and lights, and carols are sung. It's a time of joy and festivity, where people from all communities often join in the celebrations, sharing cakes and enjoying the festive spirit.
